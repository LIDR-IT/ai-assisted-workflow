# Expert Interview Guides by Domain

## General Interview Framework

### SOLER Interview Structure

**S** - Structured approach with clear objectives
**O** - Open-ended questions to encourage detailed responses
**L** - Listen actively and follow up on unexpected insights
**E** - Empathize to build rapport and trust
**R** - Record systematically with permission

### Universal Pre-Interview Protocol

#### Interview Preparation (30 minutes)

1. **Background Research:**
   - LinkedIn profile and recent activity
   - Published articles, interviews, presentations
   - Company role and responsibilities
   - Recent industry involvement or statements

2. **Question Customization:**
   - Adapt generic questions to expert's specific experience
   - Prepare follow-up questions based on their background
   - Identify potential areas of unique insight

3. **Technical Setup:**
   - Recording permission and backup methods
   - Video call quality testing
   - Note-taking template preparation

#### Interview Opening (5 minutes)

```markdown
Thank you for joining me today. I'm conducting research on [DOMAIN] to better understand [SPECIFIC RESEARCH FOCUS]. Your expertise in [THEIR SPECIFIC AREA] makes your perspective particularly valuable.

A few quick notes:

- This conversation should take about 45-60 minutes
- I'd like to record for accuracy - is that okay with you?
- Everything you share will be attributed appropriately, and I'll confirm any quotes before using them
- Feel free to flag anything as off-the-record

To start, could you briefly describe your current role and how you got involved with [DOMAIN]?
```

## Biometric/Identity Verification Domain

### Technology Expert Interview Guide

#### Expert Profile: CTOs, R&D Leads, Algorithm Developers

**Section 1: Technology Landscape (15 minutes)**

_Core Questions:_

1. "How has biometric technology fundamentally changed in the last 3 years?"
2. "What's the biggest technical challenge that's still unsolved in biometric authentication?"
3. "Where do you see the accuracy vs. speed trade-off heading?"

_Deep-Dive Follow-ups:_

- "Can you walk me through a specific example where [mentioned challenge] caused real problems?"
- "What metrics do you use internally to measure algorithm performance beyond FAR/FRR?"
- "How do you handle population bias in algorithm training data?"

**Section 2: Market Evolution (15 minutes)**

_Core Questions:_

1. "What's driving the current wave of biometric adoption?"
2. "Which use cases are harder to crack than people realize?"
3. "What's overhyped versus underhyped in the current market?"

_Deep-Dive Follow-ups:_

- "Can you give me an example of a customer who thought they wanted X but actually needed Y?"
- "What questions should buyers be asking that they're not asking?"
- "Where do you see the biggest competitive moats forming?"

**Section 3: Future Trajectory (15 minutes)**

_Core Questions:_

1. "What technology development will most impact this space in the next 2-3 years?"
2. "How will regulatory changes reshape the technical requirements?"
3. "What would you build if you were starting fresh today?"

_Expert Validation Questions:_

- "I've heard [HYPOTHESIS]. Does that align with your experience?"
- "Some experts believe [OPPOSING VIEW]. What's your take on that?"

## Advanced Interview Techniques

### Cognitive Interview Methods

#### Timeline Reconstruction

```markdown
"I'd like you to walk me through the evolution of [SPECIFIC AREA] over the last 5 years. Think about it as a timeline:

2021: What was the state of the art?
2022: What started changing?
2023: What was the inflection point?
2024: Where did things accelerate?
2025: What surprised everyone?
2026: Where are we now?

For each year, what were the key drivers of change?"
```

#### Scenario-Based Questions

```markdown
"Imagine you're advising a startup entering [DOMAIN] today with $10M in funding. What would you tell them to:

1. Avoid at all costs?
2. Invest heavily in from day one?
3. Wait and see on?
4. Partner rather than build?

Now imagine they have $100M instead - how would your advice change?"
```

#### Assumption Testing Protocol

```markdown
"I'm going to share some assumptions I'm working with. Please tell me where I'm wrong:

1. [ASSUMPTION 1]
2. [ASSUMPTION 2]
3. [ASSUMPTION 3]

For each one:

- Is this directionally correct?
- What am I missing?
- What would you modify?
- What examples support or contradict this?"
```

### Interview Quality Control

#### Real-Time Validation

During the interview, watch for:

- **Specificity Level:** Are answers concrete or generic?
- **Source Attribution:** Does expert cite specific examples or speak generally?
- **Uncertainty Acknowledgment:** Does expert acknowledge areas of uncertainty?
- **Bias Signals:** Are there obvious commercial or personal interests?

#### Post-Interview Assessment

```markdown
Interview Quality Checklist:
□ Expert provided specific, verifiable examples
□ Expert acknowledged areas of uncertainty
□ Information contradicted or confirmed other sources
□ Expert demonstrated current market awareness
□ Expert offered contrarian or non-obvious insights
□ Expert was willing to be quoted/attributed
□ Technical audio/video quality was acceptable
□ Notes captured key insights and quotes accurately
```

#### Follow-Up Protocol

```markdown
Within 24 hours:

1. Send thank-you email with key discussion points
2. Request clarification on any ambiguous statements
3. Confirm permission for specific quotes
4. Ask for referrals to other relevant experts

Within 48 hours:

1. Synthesize key insights into research database
2. Cross-reference claims against other sources
3. Identify follow-up research questions
4. Plan next interview based on insights
```

This comprehensive interview guide ensures systematic, high-quality primary research that generates unique insights and validates or challenges existing assumptions across different domains.
